What is Secondary Service Connection?

Under 38 CFR 3.310, you can receive VA disability compensation for conditions that are:

  • Caused by a service-connected disability (proximately due to)
  • Aggravated by a service-connected disability (made permanently worse)

Example: If you have service-connected PTSD and develop sleep apnea, research shows PTSD can cause sleep apnea - making it potentially claimable as a secondary condition.

How to File a Secondary Service Connection Claim

1

Gather Evidence

Get a medical opinion (nexus letter) linking your secondary condition to your primary service-connected disability.

2

File Your Claim

Submit VA Form 21-526EZ for a new claim or supplemental claim. Clearly state the condition is secondary to your existing disability.

3

Attend Your Exam

Be prepared to explain how your primary condition caused or aggravated the secondary condition. Bring supporting documentation.
